## 1) The Trinity

Christians believe in **one God in three persons**:

- **Father** – creator
- **Son** – Jesus Christ, God made human
- **Holy Spirit** – God’s presence in the world today

> 🧠 *“Three persons, one God”* — not three gods. This is a **mystery** of faith.

## 2) What was Jesus Like? – Sources for the Historical Jesus

### 📜 The 5 key sources (learn these for the exam!)

| Author | Date | Type | What we learn |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Matthew** | c. 80 AD | Gospel (Christian, Jewish audience) | Jesus is the **promised Messiah** who fulfils Old Testament prophecy. *“I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil.”* 65 OT references! |
| **Luke** | c. 85–90 AD | Gospel (Christian, Gentile audience) | Jesus came **for everyone**, not just Jews. Friend of outcasts (lepers, tax-collectors). Sympathetic to women. (e.g. Good Samaritan parable) |
| **Josephus** | c. 93 AD | Jewish historian | Non-Christian Jewish source mentioning Jesus in *Antiquities*. Important because he wasn’t a believer. |
| **Pliny the Younger** | c. 112 AD | Roman governor of Bithynia | Letter to Emperor Trajan asking how to deal with Christians – earliest **Roman policy** evidence and a description of Christian worship. |
| **Tacitus** | c. 117 AD | Roman senator/historian | Mentions Jesus’ execution under Pilate; describes Nero’s persecution of Christians. |

### 🆚 Gospel vs Biography
A **gospel** (= “good news”) is written to spread **faith** in Jesus and includes theological meaning. A **biography** is a neutral, historical life-story. Gospels select and shape events for a religious purpose.

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- Two **Christian** sources? → Matthew & Luke
- One **Jewish** non-Christian source? → Josephus
- Two **Roman** sources? → Pliny the Younger & Tacitus

## 3) Sin, Sacrifice & Salvation

### 🔑 Key Definitions

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Sin** | Action/thought that separates humans from God; behaviour against God’s laws |
| **Original sin** | Catholic doctrine: everyone is born with a built-in urge to disobey God (from Adam & Eve) |
| **Salvation** | Saving the soul — deliverance from sin and admission to heaven, brought by Jesus |
| **Sacrifice** | Offering something to God — Jesus’ death is seen as the ultimate sacrifice |

### 🐍 The Origin of Sin – Adam & Eve (Genesis 3)
- God forbade them from eating fruit of the **Tree of Knowledge of Good and Evil**.
- The serpent tempted Eve. Both ate.
- Result: **sin, evil and death** entered the world → separation from God.
- This is the basis of **original sin** in Christian teaching.

### ✝️ Holy Week – Events leading to Jesus’ death (in order!)

1. Jesus enters Jerusalem (Palm Sunday – on a donkey)
2. Drives moneylenders out of the Temple
3. Debates with **Sadducees, Pharisees** and Jewish religious leaders
4. Enemies conspire to kill him; **Judas betrays Jesus**
5. **Last Supper / Passover** with the 12 disciples
6. Arrested at **Gethsemane**
7. Trial before **Jewish authorities (Sanhedrin)** – Peter denies him 3x
8. Trial before **Pontius Pilate** – sentenced to crucifixion for **treason** (claimed to be King of the Jews)
9. Flogged and crucified
10. Dies on Good Friday – temple curtain torn in two

### 👥 Jesus’ Opponents

| Group | Who they were |
|---|---|
| **Sanhedrin** | Council of powerful Jews – High Priest, chief priests, Sadducees, scribes, Pharisees. Tried Jesus for **blasphemy**. |
| **Pharisees** | Strict Jewish teachers of the Law |
| **Sadducees** | Aristocratic priests linked to the Temple |
| **Pontius Pilate** | Roman Prefect of Judaea (26–36 AD), known for **cruelty**. Had the power to execute. |
| **Barabbas** | Rebel/murderer released by Pilate instead of Jesus |

> 💡 Pilate didn’t care about *blasphemy* (a Jewish religious charge) but was swayed because Jesus being called **“King of the Jews”** sounded like **treason** against Caesar.

### 🐑 Jesus as Sacrifice
- In the **Old Testament**, animals (lambs, goats) were sacrificed to make up for human sin.
- Jesus is called the **“Lamb of God”** because he died to atone for the sins of humanity.
- Idea of the **scapegoat** = one being takes on the sins of many.

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- Who tried Jesus first? **Sanhedrin** (religious court)
- Who actually sentenced him to death? **Pontius Pilate**
- Who was released instead of Jesus? **Barabbas**
- Why is Jesus called the “Lamb of God”? Because he was **sacrificed for the sins of humanity**.

## 4) The Resurrection

### 🪦 The Burial
- Body placed in tomb of **Joseph of Arimathea** (a believer).
- Buried Friday, stone rolled across the entrance.
- **Sabbath** (Saturday) = day of rest, so nothing could be done.

### 🌅 Easter Sunday
- **Mary Magdalene** and the other Mary went to the tomb at dawn.
- Earthquake; an **angel** rolled the stone away – guards collapsed in fear.
- Angel: *“He is not here; he has risen.”*
- Jesus appears to the women, then later to disciples (including **doubting Thomas**).

### 🔍 Did Jesus Really Rise? – Arguments For and Against

| ❌ Against the Resurrection | ✅ For the Resurrection |
|---|---|
| Jesus only fainted on the cross and woke up in the tomb | A Roman officer **checked Jesus was dead** before releasing the body |
| The disciples stole the body and made it up | Most disciples **died as martyrs** – why die for a lie? |
| The women went to the **wrong tomb** | The women had **watched Joseph** place the body |
| The women imagined the angel out of grief | The **authorities couldn’t produce the body** |

### 👤 The Importance of Peter
- Originally abandoned Jesus and denied him 3 times.
- After the resurrection, became leader of the church in Jerusalem.
- Died as a **martyr** in Rome.
- His **transformation** is strong evidence for many Christians.

### 📖 St Paul – 1 Corinthians 15
> *“If Christ has not been raised, your faith is futile.”*
- The resurrection is **central** to Christian faith. Without it, there is no Christianity.

### 💫 Why is the Resurrection Important Today?
1. Shows the **power of good over evil**.
2. Christians don’t need to fear death – they too can be resurrected.
3. Assures Christians their **sins will be forgiven**.
4. **Validates** Jesus’ teachings.
5. Proves Jesus was **God incarnate**, not just a human.

### 📅 Holy Week at a Glance

| Day | Event |
|---|---|
| Palm Sunday | Jesus rides into Jerusalem on a donkey |
| Monday | Turns over the moneylenders’ tables |
| Tuesday | Teaches in the Temple |
| Wednesday | With Martha & Mary in Bethany; Judas agrees to betray |
| Maundy Thursday | Last Supper, arrest, trials begin |
| Good Friday | Pilate sentences him; flogged, crucified, dies |
| Saturday | Sabbath – body in tomb |
| Easter Sunday | **Resurrection** – tomb empty |

## 5) Constantine and Three Key Events in Christian History

### ⚔️ A) Roman Persecution of Christians

**Why were Christians persecuted?**
- Refused to worship Roman gods or pray for the Empire.
- Did not have the Jewish exemption from emperor worship.
- Romans believed disrespecting the gods would bring disaster (= need to **propitiate the gods**).
- Made an easy **scapegoat** for crises.

**Three case studies to remember:**

| Person | Date | What happened |
|---|---|---|
| **Christians under Nero** | 64 AD | After the **Great Fire of Rome**, Nero blamed Christians. They were burned, fed to dogs, crucified – used as “human torches” in his garden. (Source: **Tacitus, Annals**) |
| **Polycarp** | c. 155–160 AD | Bishop of Smyrna, aged 86. Refused to swear by Caesar: *“For 86 years I have been his servant, and he has done me no wrong.”* **Burned alive**. Earliest Christian martyrdom account outside the New Testament. |
| **Vibia Perpetua** | 7 March 203 AD | Young noblewoman in Carthage, recently married, nursing a baby. Refused to recant even when her father begged. Killed in the arena with her slave **Felicity** and others. *“I am a Christian.”* |

**Why did Christianity survive?**
- Martyrs inspired others — *“the blood of the martyrs is the seed of the Church.”*
- Spread through Roman roads and trade.
- Strong sense of community and care for the poor.

### 👑 B) Constantine’s Conversion (312 AD)
- Roman Emperor who converted to Christianity after a **vision of a cross before the Battle of the Milvian Bridge**.
- Issued the **Edict of Milan (313 AD)** legalising Christianity.
- This **transformed** Christianity from a persecuted minority into the religion of the Empire.

### 🪨 C) St Peter & St Paul – Founders of the Early Church

#### St Peter
- Real name **Simon**, renamed **Petros** (= “rock”) by Jesus.
- Fisherman, brother of Andrew, one of Jesus’ closest disciples.
- Initially **denied Jesus 3 times**.
- After the resurrection: leader of the church in Jerusalem.
- **Acts 10 – Peter and Cornelius**: vision allows Gentiles into the Christian movement (no need to become Jews first).
- Died as a martyr; **Catholics see him as the first Pope**.

#### St Paul (formerly Saul)
- Born in **Tarsus** (modern Turkey), a **Jewish Roman citizen**.
- **Persecuted Christians** at first.
- **Road to Damascus** (~35–36 AD): vision of Jesus → conversion.
- Travelled for 20+ years across Syria, Greece spreading the gospel.
- Wrote **13 of the 27 books of the New Testament** (epistles/letters).
- Faced beatings, prison, shipwrecks, mockery for the faith.
- Hugely shaped Christian doctrine (e.g. **original sin** as we know it).

> ✏️ Common exam essay: *“Whose contribution was greater — Peter or Paul?”* Argue both sides! Peter = continuity (rock of the Church, link to Jesus), Paul = expansion (took Christianity to the Gentile world, wrote much of the NT).

### ⛪ D) The Great Schism (1054 AD)

The split between the **Roman Catholic Church (West)** and the **Eastern Orthodox Church (East)**.

| Cause | Type | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **Filioque** clause | 🟦 Doctrine | West added “and the Son” to the Creed (re: role of Jesus in the Trinity). East said you can’t change the original wording. |
| **Papal Authority** | 🟥 Power | West said the **Pope of Rome** had supreme authority. East said all 5 patriarchs (Rome, Constantinople, Alexandria, Antioch, Jerusalem) were equal. |
| **Language** | 🟩 Practical | West used **Latin**, East used **Greek**. |
| **Importance of Constantinople** | 🟥 Power | East argued Constantinople was equal to Rome; West disagreed. |
| **Leavened/Unleavened bread** | 🟩 Practical | East: **leavened** (yeast = life/resurrection). West: **unleavened** (like at the Last Supper). |

> 🔥 In **1054**, Cardinal Humbert excommunicated the Orthodox Church during Mass at Hagia Sophia in Constantinople: *“May God look upon us and judge.”*

**Colour-coded key:** 🟥 Power · 🟦 Doctrine · 🟩 Practicalities

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- 3 famous martyrs? **Christians under Nero, Polycarp, Vibia Perpetua**
- Date of Constantine’s legalisation of Christianity? **313 AD (Edict of Milan)**
- Date and name of the East-West split? **1054 — The Great Schism**
- How many NT books did Paul write? **13**

## 6) Catholicism

### 👑 The Papacy
- **Pope** = leader of the Roman Catholic Church and **Bishop of Rome**.
- Believed to be the **direct successor of St Peter**.
- Current Pope: **Pope Leo XIV**.
- **Protestants do not accept** the Pope’s authority.
- **Office of the Pope** = the **papacy**.

### 🗳️ Electing a Pope (the Conclave)
- Held in the **Sistine Chapel** (checked for hidden microphones!).
- Voters: cardinals **under 80** from the **College of Cardinals**.
- Each writes their choice on a ballot under *“Eligo in Summum Pontificem”* (“I elect as supreme pontiff”).
- Need a **2/3 majority** to win.
- Ballots burned: **black smoke** = no Pope yet; **white smoke** = new Pope chosen!
- Cardinals **cannot vote for themselves**.

### 🌟 Why is the Papacy Important?
1. An **extra source of authority** alongside the Bible.
2. Represents a **direct line back to Jesus** through Peter.
3. Politically active – tackles injustice and poverty.
4. The Catholic Church is the **largest Christian denomination** worldwide (1.2 billion+).

### 🕯️ The Seven Sacraments
A **sacrament** = an outward expression of an inward truth; a ritual that strengthens faith and brings God’s grace.

1. **Baptism**
2. **Confirmation**
3. **Eucharist** (Mass / Holy Communion)
4. **Reconciliation** (Confession)
5. **Marriage**
6. **Holy Orders** (becoming a priest)
7. **Anointing of the Sick**

> 🧠 Mnemonic: **B**ig **C**ats **E**at **R**ed **M**ice **H**iding **A**round → Baptism, Confirmation, Eucharist, Reconciliation, Marriage, Holy Orders, Anointing.

#### Different Christian views
- **Protestants**: only some sacraments (often just Baptism & Eucharist – the two Jesus performed).
- **Quakers**: no sacraments needed – rituals aren’t required to communicate with God.

### 💧 Baptism

| Type | Who | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Infant baptism** (Catholic) | Babies | To wash away **original sin** as soon as possible. Necessary for salvation. |
| **Believers’ baptism** (e.g. Baptists) | Adults / older children | Person must understand and choose – like Jesus’ own baptism. |

**Symbols of Baptism:**
- 💧 **Water** – new life in Christ.
- 🛢️ **Holy oil (Chrism)** – strength, healing, gift of the Holy Spirit.
- ⚪ **White garment** – purity; new identity in the community.
- 🕯️ **Candle** – Jesus is the light of the world (lit from the Paschal candle).

### 🍞 The Eucharist
- Commemorates the **Last Supper** – Jesus gave bread and wine as symbols of his body and blood.
- Different names: **Eucharist / Mass / Holy Communion / Divine Liturgy**.
- Catholics believe in **transubstantiation** – the bread and wine **literally become** the body and blood of Christ when blessed by the priest.
- Celebrated **every week** (often daily) in Catholic churches.

> 📖 *“This is my body which is for you; do this as a memorial of me.”* (1 Cor. 11:23-29)

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- Who is the Pope a successor of? **St Peter**
- 7 sacraments? **B-C-E-R-M-H-A** (see mnemonic)
- What is **transubstantiation**? Catholic belief that bread/wine literally become Jesus’ body/blood.
- What does **white smoke** from the Sistine Chapel mean? **A new Pope has been chosen.**
