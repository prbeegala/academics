## 1) Beliefs about G-d

### 🕎 Core Beliefs
- **Monotheism** – there is **only one G-d**, the creator of all things.
- G-d is:
  - **Omnipotent** (all-powerful)
  - **Omniscient** (all-knowing)
  - **Omnibenevolent** (all-good)
  - **Eternal** (has always existed and always will)
- G-d cannot be physically represented (no statues or images).

### ✍️ Why "G-d" and not "God"?
Out of **respect**, many Jews avoid writing G-d's name in full because:
- His name is considered too **holy** to be erased or destroyed.
- Writing it incompletely shows reverence.

### 🤝 The Covenant
A **covenant** = a sacred agreement between G-d and his people.
- **Abraham**: G-d promised to make his descendants a great nation; Abraham promised to follow G-d.
- **Moses**: G-d gave the **Torah and Ten Commandments** in exchange for obedience.

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- Three "omni" qualities of G-d? **Omnipotent, omniscient, omnibenevolent**
- Why is G-d's name written incompletely? Out of **respect** for its holiness
- Who were the first two people to make a covenant with G-d? **Abraham** and **Moses**

## 2) Moses

### 👤 Key Facts
- The **most important prophet** in Judaism.
- Born in Egypt during a time when Hebrews were **enslaved**.
- Saved as a baby by being placed in a basket on the river — found by Pharaoh's daughter and raised in the palace.

### 🌊 The Exodus
- G-d called Moses through the **burning bush** to free the Hebrews.
- Moses confronted Pharaoh: *“Let my people go!”*
- G-d sent the **10 plagues** on Egypt.
- The Hebrews escaped through the **parted Red Sea**.

### 📜 Mount Sinai
- After the Exodus, Moses received the **Torah** and the **Ten Commandments** on **Mount Sinai**.
- This was the renewal of the **covenant** between G-d and his people.

### 📋 The Ten Commandments (briefly)
1. I am the Lord your G-d
2. No other gods
3. Do not misuse G-d's name
4. Keep the Sabbath holy
5. Honour your father and mother
6. Do not murder
7. Do not commit adultery
8. Do not steal
9. Do not lie about others
10. Do not covet (envy) what others have

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- How did G-d call Moses? Through the **burning bush**
- Where did Moses receive the Torah? **Mount Sinai**
- How many commandments? **Ten**
- What festival remembers the Exodus? **Pesach (Passover)**

## 3) Jewish Law – Written and Oral

### 📜 The Written Torah
The **first 5 books** of the Hebrew Bible (the **Tanakh**):

| Book | Topic |
|---|---|
| **Genesis** | Creation; Adam & Eve; Abraham |
| **Exodus** | Moses; escape from Egypt; Ten Commandments |
| **Leviticus** | Laws and rituals |
| **Numbers** | Wandering in the wilderness |
| **Deuteronomy** | Moses's final teachings |

The Torah contains **613 mitzvot** (commandments) — laws covering food, prayer, behaviour and more.

### 🗣️ The Oral Torah
- Explanations and traditions **passed down by rabbis** for generations.
- Eventually written down as:
  - **Mishnah** (~200 CE) – core summary of oral law.
  - **Talmud** (~500 CE) – the Mishnah + rabbi commentary.

### 🥩 Examples of Jewish Law
- **Kosher food laws**: e.g. no pork, no shellfish, meat and milk not eaten together.
- **Shabbat laws**: no work from Friday evening to Saturday evening.
- **Festival laws**: how to celebrate Pesach, Yom Kippur etc.

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- What does "Torah" mean? **"Teaching" or "Law"**
- 5 books of the Torah? **Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy**
- What is the Talmud? Mishnah + **rabbinic commentary**
- How many mitzvot? **613**

## 4) Worship

### 🕍 The Synagogue
- Jewish **place of worship** and community.
- Contains:
  - **Ark (Aron Hakodesh)** – cabinet holding the Torah scrolls.
  - **Bimah** – raised platform from where the Torah is read.
  - **Ner Tamid** – the “eternal light” always burning.
  - **Star of David / Menorah** – Jewish symbols.
- **Men and women may sit separately** in Orthodox synagogues.

### 👤 The Rabbi
- A **teacher and leader** of the Jewish community.
- Leads worship, teaches the Torah, advises on Jewish law.
- Not a priest – ordinary men (and women in Reform Judaism) can become rabbis after study.

### 🕯️ Shabbat (the Sabbath)
- Day of **rest and worship**: Friday sunset to Saturday sunset.
- Remembers G-d resting on the 7th day of creation.
- Begins with the lighting of **Shabbat candles** and the **Kiddush** (blessing over wine).
- **Challah bread** is shared.
- No work is allowed: cooking, writing, driving, switching on lights etc. are forbidden.

### 🙏 Prayer
- **Three times a day** in Orthodox Judaism (morning, afternoon, evening).
- Prayers come from the **Siddur** (prayer book).
- Many men wear:
  - **Kippah** – skullcap (humility before G-d).
  - **Tallit** – prayer shawl.
  - **Tefillin** – small leather boxes with scripture, worn on arm and forehead during weekday morning prayer.

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- Place of worship? **Synagogue**
- Who leads it? **Rabbi**
- Day of rest? **Shabbat** – Friday sunset to Saturday sunset
- Prayer book? **Siddur**

## 5) Yom Kippur – The Day of Atonement

### 📅 Key Facts
- The **most holy day** in the Jewish year.
- A day of **fasting, prayer and asking forgiveness** for sins committed in the past year.
- Falls in **September/October** (10 days after Rosh Hashanah, the Jewish New Year).

### 🤲 What Happens?
- **25-hour fast** – no food or water.
- Jews wear **white** (symbolises purity).
- Long synagogue services with prayers of repentance.
- The **shofar** (ram's horn) is blown at the end.
- The aim is **teshuvah** = *“return”* to G-d through repentance.

### 🌟 Why is it important?
- It is the chance to **wipe the slate clean** before G-d.
- Encourages reflection on behaviour and **making peace with others** (you must seek forgiveness from those you have wronged before G-d will forgive you).

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- What does Yom Kippur mean? **Day of Atonement**
- How long is the fast? **25 hours**
- What is **teshuvah**? **Return / repentance**
- What instrument is blown at the end? **Shofar (ram's horn)**

## 6) Pesach (Passover)

### 📅 Key Facts
- A **spring festival** lasting **7–8 days**.
- Remembers the **Exodus from Egypt** under Moses.
- Why "Passover"? Because the **Angel of Death "passed over"** Jewish homes (marked with lamb's blood) during the 10th plague.

### 🍽️ The Seder Meal
A special meal on the **first evening** of Pesach where the story of the Exodus is retold.

| Symbolic food | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Matzah** (unleavened bread) | Jews left Egypt in such a hurry the bread didn't have time to rise |
| **Maror** (bitter herbs) | The bitterness of slavery |
| **Charoset** (sweet apple/nut paste) | The mortar Hebrew slaves used to build for the Egyptians |
| **Karpas** (vegetable, e.g. parsley) | Spring / new beginnings; dipped in salt water for tears |
| **Salt water** | The tears of slaves |
| **Z'roa** (lamb shank bone) | The Passover lamb sacrifice |
| **Beitzah** (egg) | Mourning, and the cycle of life |

### 📖 The Haggadah
The **special book** read at the Seder, which retells the Exodus story.

### 🍞 No Leavened Bread!
For all 7-8 days, Jews avoid any food with yeast (**chametz**) — homes are cleaned thoroughly to remove every trace.

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- What event does Pesach remember? **The Exodus from Egypt**
- Why "Passover"? The Angel of Death **passed over** Jewish homes
- Special meal? **The Seder**
- Bread eaten during Pesach? **Matzah** (unleavened)
- Book read at the Seder? **The Haggadah**
