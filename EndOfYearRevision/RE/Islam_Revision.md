## 1) The Life of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

### 📅 Key Dates (memorise these!)

| Year | Event |
|---|---|
| **c. 570** | Birth of Muhammad in Mecca |
| **610** | First revelation at the cave of **Hira** |
| **613** | Begins preaching Islam publicly |
| **619** | Death of wife **Khadija** & uncle **Abu Talib** (“Year of Sorrow”) |
| **622** | **The Hijra** – migration to Yathrib (later Medina). Muslim calendar begins (1 AH) |
| **630** | Muslim victory over Quraysh; peaceful return to Mecca |
| **632** | Last Sermon and death of Muhammad |

### 👶 Early Life
- Looked after by a **Bedouin foster-mother**.
- Orphaned at **6**, raised by his uncle **Abu Talib** from age **8**.
- Worked as a **trader** – known for **honesty and integrity**.
- A Christian monk **Bahira** recognised signs of prophethood.
- **Khadija**, a 40-year-old widow, hired him → later proposed marriage. Happy marriage, 6 children.

### 🕋 Mecca Before Islam
- Religious city full of many religions.
- The **Ka’ba** housed many **idols**.
- Important **pilgrimage** site.

### 🌙 The Night of Power (610 CE)
- During Ramadan, in the cave of **Hira**, aged 40.
- The angel **Gabriel** appeared and commanded **“Read!”** (*Iqra!*).
- First revelation of the Qur’an: *“Read in the name of your Lord who created…”*
- Muhammad was scared; **Khadija comforted him** and her Christian cousin confirmed he was a prophet.

### 📢 Preaching and Persecution
- Muhammad preached **monotheism** – stop worshipping idols.
- Angered the **Quraysh** tribe who controlled access to the Ka’ba (and made money from pilgrims).
- Protected by his uncle Abu Talib until **619** – then attacks increased.

### 🐪 The Hijra (622 CE) – the migration
- Pilgrims from **Yathrib** (300 km away) invited Muhammad to come.
- Muhammad sent followers ahead, left last with **Abu Bakr** (close friend) and **Ali** (cousin).
- **Ali tricked the Quraysh** by sleeping in Muhammad’s bed.
- This journey = **the Hijra**. Muslim calendar starts here: **622 = 1 AH** (After Hijra).

### 🕌 Medina (Yathrib renamed)
- Muhammad founded the **first Muslim community** (*ummah*).
- City renamed **Madinah-al-Nabi** (City of the Prophet).
- The **first mosque** was built next to his house.
- He taught **everyone is equal before God**.
- Mended his own clothes, did his own shopping – led humbly.
- Became Medina’s **spiritual AND political leader**.
- A series of **battles** with Mecca followed.

### 🕊️ Return to Mecca (630 CE)
- Muhammad returned victorious – **destroyed the idols** in the Ka’ba.
- Made it the **centre of worship of Allah**.

### 📿 Last Years (632 CE)
- Final pilgrimage to Mecca → **Last Sermon**: urged unity among Muslims.
- Said he was leaving them two things to follow:
  - **Sunni view**: the **Qur’an** and his **example (Sunnah)**.
  - **Shi’a view**: the **Qur’an** and his **family** (with **Ali** as his successor).
- Died in the arms of his wife **Aisha** in Medina.
- Tomb is in **Medina** today.
- Known as **the Seal of the Prophets** – the **last** prophet.

> 📖 *“Muhammad is not a father of any of your men, but he is a messenger of Allah and the last of the prophets.”* (Qur’an 33:40)

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- First revelation? **610 CE, cave of Hira**
- Hijra year? **622 = 1 AH**
- Wife and uncle who died in 619? **Khadija and Abu Talib**
- Title given to Muhammad as the last prophet? **Seal of the Prophets**
- Sunni vs Shia split — what did Muhammad leave? Sunni: **Qur’an + Sunnah**; Shi’a: **Qur’an + family (Ali)**

## 2) The Qur’an and Hadiths

> ⚠️ *No specific powerpoint provided – revise alongside class notes.*

| Source | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Qur’an** | The literal **word of Allah**, revealed through **Gabriel to Muhammad** over 23 years. Written in **Arabic**. **114 chapters** (*surahs*). Considered perfect, unchangeable. |
| **Hadith** | **Reports of what Muhammad said, did or approved of.** Collected by scholars after his death. Used (with the Qur’an) to guide Muslim life. Together with his actions = the **Sunnah**. |

### 🔑 Key Difference
- **Qur’an** = **God’s words** (highest authority).
- **Hadith** = **Muhammad’s words and actions** (second source of guidance).

### 📚 How Muslims Use Them
- The **Qur'an** is read in Arabic during prayers (5 daily prayers).
- Memorising the Qur'an is highly respected — someone who does so is a *Hafiz*.
- The **Hadith** helps explain *how* to live by the Qur'an in everyday life (e.g. how to pray, how to behave).

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- How many surahs in the Qur'an? **114**
- In what language was the Qur'an revealed? **Arabic**
- Who collected the Hadith? **Muslim scholars after Muhammad's death**
- What is the **Sunnah**? Muhammad's example (his words + actions)

## 3) The Rightly Guided Caliphs

> ⚠️ *No specific powerpoint provided – revise alongside class notes.*

After Muhammad’s death (632 CE), four close companions led the Muslim community.
**Sunni** Muslims call them the **Rashidun** (“Rightly Guided”).

| # | Caliph | Years | Known for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Abu Bakr** | 632–634 | Muhammad’s closest friend; reunited the Arabian tribes; began compiling the Qur’an. |
| 2 | **Umar (ibn al-Khattab)** | 634–644 | Massive expansion of Islam (Persia, Egypt, Syria); strong administrator and just ruler. |
| 3 | **Uthman (ibn Affan)** | 644–656 | Produced the **standardised written Qur’an**. Assassinated. |
| 4 | **Ali (ibn Abi Talib)** | 656–661 | Muhammad’s cousin and son-in-law. **Shi’a Muslims** believe he should have been first — this is the origin of the **Sunni–Shi’a split**. |

### 🧠 Mnemonic for the order
**A**bu Bakr, **U**mar, **U**thman, **A**li → **AUUA** ("**A** **U**niversity **U**nion **A**ssociation")

### ⚖️ Sunni vs Shi'a (origin of the split)
- **Sunnis** (~85–90% of Muslims) accept all four Rashidun caliphs as legitimate leaders.
- **Shi'as** (~10–15%) believe **Ali** should have succeeded Muhammad immediately, because Muhammad chose family over companions.

### 🎯 Quick Recall
- Who was the **first** caliph? **Abu Bakr**
- Who **standardised the Qur'an**? **Uthman**
- Who was Muhammad's cousin and 4th caliph? **Ali**
- Why is Ali special to Shi'a Muslims? They believe he should have been the **first** caliph.

| **Gabriel (Jibril)** | The angel who delivered God's revelation to Muhammad |
| **Hadith** | Sayings and actions of Muhammad |
| **Hijra** | Muhammad's migration to Medina (622 CE) |
| **Ka'ba** | Cube-shaped shrine in Mecca, holiest site in Islam |
| **Mecca / Makkah** | Birthplace of Muhammad; holiest city in Islam |
| **Medina / Madinah** | The “City of the Prophet”, where Muhammad founded the first Muslim community |
| **Prophet** | A person chosen by God to deliver a message |
| **Quraysh** | The powerful tribe of Mecca that opposed Muhammad |
| **Qur'an** | Holy book of Islam – the literal word of Allah |
| **Ramadan** | Holy month of fasting; the month of the first revelation |
| **Rashidun** | The four "Rightly Guided" Caliphs |

| **Shi'a** | Muslims who believe Ali should have been Muhammad's immediate successor |
| **Sunnah** | The example/way of Muhammad |
