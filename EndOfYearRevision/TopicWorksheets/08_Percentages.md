### 🔑 Key Ideas

- **% of an amount:** divide by 100, then multiply. For example, 18% of £250 = 0.18 × 250.
- **Increase/decrease via multipliers:** increase by p% means ×(1 + p/100); decrease by p% means ×(1 − p/100).
- **Reverse %:** if you know the final amount, **DIVIDE by the multiplier** to get the original.
- **Compound interest formula:** Final = Original × (1 + r/100)ⁿ.
- **Percentage change formula:** percentage change = (new − old) ÷ old × 100.

---

### ✏️ Worked Examples

**Example 1 — Reverse percentage**

After a 20% sale discount, a coat costs £64. What was the original price?

A 20% discount means the customer pays 80% of the original price.

Multiplier = 0.80

So: final price = original price × 0.80

£64 = original price × 0.80

Original price = £64 ÷ 0.80 = £80

**Important:** do not add 20% of £64. The £64 is already the reduced amount, not the original.

**Answer: £80**

---

**Example 2 — Compound interest**

A savings account contains £600 and earns 4% compound interest each year for 3 years. How much is in the account at the end?

A 4% increase means multiplier = 1.04

Final = Original × 1.04³

Final = £600 × 1.04³

Final = £600 × 1.124864 = £674.9184

**Answer: £674.92**
