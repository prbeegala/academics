### 🔑 Key Ideas

- Circumference of a circle: C = 2πr = πd
- Area of a circle: A = πr²
- Arc length: (θ ÷ 360) × 2πr, where θ is in degrees
- Sector area: (θ ÷ 360) × πr², where θ is in degrees
- Perimeter of a sector: arc length + 2r
- For a semicircle, use half the area or circumference of the full circle.
- For an annulus (ring), subtract the smaller circle area from the larger circle area.

---

### ✏️ Worked Examples

**Example 1 — Sector area and arc length**

A sector has radius 6 cm and central angle 120°. Find its arc length and area, leaving answers in terms of π and giving decimal answers.

Arc length = (120 ÷ 360) × 2π × 6  
= 1/3 × 12π  
= 4π cm  
≈ 12.57 cm

Sector area = (120 ÷ 360) × π × 6²  
= 1/3 × 36π  
= 12π cm²  
≈ 37.70 cm²

**Example 2 — Composite shape**

A rectangle is 8 cm wide and 5 cm high. A semicircle is placed on top of the rectangle, with diameter 8 cm. Find the total area.

Rectangle area = 8 × 5 = 40 cm²  
Semicircle radius = 8 ÷ 2 = 4 cm  
Semicircle area = 1/2 × π × 4² = 8π cm²  
Total area = 40 + 8π cm²  
≈ 65.13 cm²
