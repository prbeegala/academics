### 🔑 Key Ideas

- **Theoretical probability** = favourable outcomes ÷ total outcomes.
- **Experimental probability / relative frequency** = number of times the event happened ÷ number of trials.
- **Expected frequency** = probability × number of trials.
- Probabilities for all possible outcomes add to 1. Also, P(not A) = 1 − P(A).
- **Tree diagrams:** multiply along branches, then add the ends for the outcomes you want.

---

### ✏️ Worked Examples

**Example 1 — Two dice sample space**

Two fair six-sided dice are rolled. Find P(sum = 7).

Possible sums of 7: 1 + 6, 2 + 5, 3 + 4, 4 + 3, 5 + 2, 6 + 1.  
There are 6 favourable outcomes out of 36 total outcomes.

Answer: P(sum = 7) = 6/36 = 1/6

**Example 2 — Experimental probability and expected frequency**

A spinner is spun 80 times. It lands on blue 18 times.

Experimental probability of blue = 18/80 = 9/40 = 0.225.  
If the same spinner is spun 200 times, expected number of blues ≈ 0.225 × 200 = 45.

Answer: Experimental probability = 9/40; expected blues ≈ 45
