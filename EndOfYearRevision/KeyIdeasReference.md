## 🧮 ALGEBRA

### Inequalities
- Solve like an equation — do the same operation to both sides.
- **Flip the sign** when you multiply or divide by a **negative** number (`<` ↔ `>`, `≤` ↔ `≥`).
- Number-line notation: ○ = endpoint **not** included (`<`, `>`); ● = endpoint **included** (`≤`, `≥`).
- Shading: left for "less than", right for "greater than".
- For integer solutions, solve first, then list only whole numbers that fit.

### Indices (powers)
- `aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ`
- `aᵐ ÷ aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ`
- `(aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ`
- `a⁰ = 1` (anything to the power 0 is 1)
- `a⁻ⁿ = 1 / aⁿ` (negative power = reciprocal)
- `a^(1/n) = ⁿ√a`,  `a^(m/n) = (ⁿ√a)ᵐ`
- `(ab)ⁿ = aⁿbⁿ`,  `(a/b)ⁿ = aⁿ/bⁿ`

### Expansion of brackets
- **Single bracket:** `a(b + c) = ab + ac`
- **Double bracket (FOIL):** `(x + a)(x + b) = x² + (a+b)x + ab`
- **Squaring a bracket:** `(x + a)² = x² + 2ax + a²`
- **Difference of two squares:** `(x + a)(x − a) = x² − a²`

### Factorisation
- **Common factor first:** `6x² + 9x = 3x(2x + 3)`.
- **Quadratic `x² + bx + c`:** find two numbers that **multiply to c** and **add to b**.
- **Difference of two squares:** `x² − a² = (x + a)(x − a)`.
- **Quadratic with leading coefficient `a` (`ax² + bx + c`):** split the middle term — find two numbers that multiply to `a × c` and add to `b`.

### Quadratic equations
- Standard form: `ax² + bx + c = 0`.
- **Solve by factorising:** factor, then set each bracket = 0.
- **Quadratic formula:**
  
  `x = ( −b ± √(b² − 4ac) ) / (2a)`
- The **discriminant** `b² − 4ac` tells you how many real solutions:
  - `> 0` → two solutions
  - `= 0` → one repeated solution
  - `< 0` → no real solutions

### Surds
- `√(ab) = √a × √b`,   `√(a/b) = √a / √b`
- Simplify by pulling out the largest **square** factor: `√50 = √(25 × 2) = 5√2`.
- Add/subtract only **like surds**: `3√2 + 5√2 = 8√2`.
- **Rationalise the denominator:** multiply top and bottom by the surd, e.g. `1/√3 = √3/3`.

### Sequences & nth term
- **Linear (arithmetic):** common difference `d`. nth term = `a + (n − 1)d` (or `dn + c`).
- **Quadratic:** second differences are constant. nth term contains an `n²` term; the coefficient of `n²` = (second difference) ÷ 2.
- **Geometric:** common ratio `r`. nth term = `a × r^(n − 1)`.
- To check a value is in the sequence, set the nth term = that value and solve for `n`; `n` must be a positive whole number.

### Line equations (straight lines)
- **Slope-intercept form:** `y = mx + c`, where `m` = gradient, `c` = y-intercept.
- **Gradient between two points:** `m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁)`.
- **Parallel lines** have the **same gradient**.
- **Perpendicular lines:** gradients multiply to `−1` (one is the **negative reciprocal** of the other).
- **Intersection of two lines:** solve the equations simultaneously.
- A line through `(x₁, y₁)` with gradient `m`: `y − y₁ = m(x − x₁)`.

### Trial and Improvement
- Find two integers where the function is **too low** for one and **too high** for the other — the root is between them.
- Try values to 1 d.p. between them; track each trial in a **table**.
- Decide each result is "too high" or "too low" and narrow the interval.
- **To round to 1 d.p.:** test the midpoint (e.g. 3.55); if too low, the answer rounds **up** to 3.6.

## 📐 GEOMETRY

### Congruence and Similarity
- **Congruent** = same shape **and** size (allowed: rotate / reflect / translate).
- **Similar** = same shape, possibly different size; corresponding angles equal, corresponding sides in the same ratio.
- **Triangle congruence rules:** SSS, SAS, ASA / AAS, RHS.
- **Scale factors for similar shapes:**
  - Length scale factor: `k`
  - Area scale factor: `k²`
  - Volume scale factor: `k³`
- Always match **corresponding** sides before writing a ratio.

### Loci & Constructions
- **Distance r from a point** → circle of radius r.
- **Distance r from a line segment** → "stadium": parallel lines + semicircles at ends.
- **Equidistant from two points** → perpendicular bisector of the segment joining them.
- **Equidistant from two lines** → angle bisector.
- **Constructions** (compass + ruler only):
  - Perpendicular bisector: equal arcs from each endpoint, join intersections.
  - Angle bisector: arc cuts both arms; equal arcs from those points; join.
  - Perpendicular from a point to a line: arcs from the point cut the line twice; bisect those.
  - Equilateral triangle: arcs of radius AB from A and B; join intersection.
- For **region** problems: draw all boundaries, decide which side satisfies each condition, shade the overlap.

### Circle Theorems
1. **Angle at the centre = 2 × angle at the circumference** (same arc/chord).
2. **Angles in the same segment are equal.**
3. **Angle in a semicircle = 90°** (Thales).
4. **Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral add to 180°.**
5. **Tangent ⟂ radius** at the point of contact.
6. **Two tangents from an external point are equal in length.**
7. *(Stretch)* **Alternate segment theorem:** angle between tangent and chord = angle in the opposite segment.

### Circles — measurements
- Circumference: `C = 2πr = πd`
- Area: `A = πr²`
- **Arc length** = `(θ / 360) × 2πr`
- **Sector area** = `(θ / 360) × πr²`
- **Sector perimeter** = arc length + 2r
- **Semicircle:** half the full area / circumference, plus the diameter for perimeter.
- **Annulus (ring):** big circle area − small circle area.

### Geometry using algebra
- Set up an equation using a known fact, then solve:
  - **Angles on a straight line** = 180°.
  - **Angles around a point** = 360°.
  - **Angles in a triangle** = 180°; **in a quadrilateral** = 360°.
  - **Sum of interior angles of an n-gon** = `(n − 2) × 180°`.
  - **Exterior angle of a regular n-gon** = `360° / n`.
- **Pythagoras:** `a² + b² = c²` (c = hypotenuse).
- **Isosceles** triangles → two equal sides ⇒ two equal base angles.

## ⚖️ NUMBER, RATIO & PROPORTION

### Converting Units
- **Length:** 10 mm = 1 cm, 100 cm = 1 m, 1000 m = 1 km.
- **Mass:** 1000 mg = 1 g, 1000 g = 1 kg, 1000 kg = 1 tonne.
- **Capacity:** 1000 ml = 1 L; 1 cm³ = 1 ml; 1000 cm³ = 1 L; 1 m³ = 1000 L.
- **Area is squared:** 1 m² = 10 000 cm² (NOT 100); 1 cm² = 100 mm².
- **Volume is cubed:** 1 m³ = 1 000 000 cm³.
- **Time:** 1 h = 60 min = 3600 s. To get **decimal hours** from minutes: ÷ 60.

### Compound Units
- **Speed–Distance–Time:**  
  `Speed = Distance ÷ Time`,  `Distance = Speed × Time`,  `Time = Distance ÷ Speed`.
- **Density–Mass–Volume:**  
  `Density = Mass ÷ Volume`,  `Mass = Density × Volume`,  `Volume = Mass ÷ Density`.
- **Pressure–Force–Area:**  
  `Pressure = Force ÷ Area`,  `Force = Pressure × Area`,  `Area = Force ÷ Pressure`.
- **Conversions to remember:**
  - km/h ↔ m/s: **÷ 3.6** (km/h → m/s),  **× 3.6** (m/s → km/h).
  - 1 g/cm³ = 1000 kg/m³.
  - 1 N/m² = 1 Pa.
  - Use `g = 10 N/kg` for weight = mass × g unless told otherwise.

### Percentages
- **% of an amount:** divide the % by 100, then multiply. e.g. 18% of 250 = `0.18 × 250`.
- **Increase by p%:** × `(1 + p/100)`.   **Decrease by p%:** × `(1 − p/100)`.
- **Reverse %:** if you know the **final** amount, **divide** by the multiplier to get the original.
- **Compound interest:** `Final = Original × (1 + r/100)ⁿ`.
- **Percentage change:** `(new − old) ÷ old × 100`.

## 🎲 PROBABILITY

- **Theoretical probability** = favourable outcomes ÷ total outcomes.
- **Experimental probability** (relative frequency) = times event happened ÷ number of trials.
- **Expected frequency** = probability × number of trials.
- All probabilities sum to 1: `P(not A) = 1 − P(A)`.
- **Tree diagrams:** **multiply along** the branches, **add the ends** for the outcomes you want.
- Probabilities are between 0 and 1 — give as a fraction (simplest form), decimal, or percentage; **be consistent** in one question.
