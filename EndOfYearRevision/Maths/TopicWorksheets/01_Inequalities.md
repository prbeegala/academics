### 🔑 Key Ideas

- Treat an inequality like an equation: do the same operation to both sides.
- If you multiply or divide by a negative number, flip the sign: < becomes >, and ≤ becomes ≥.
- Open circle ○ means the endpoint is not included: < or >.
- Closed circle ● means the endpoint is included: ≤ or ≥.
- Number line shading goes left for “less than” and right for “greater than”. Example: x ≤ 3 is `←————●————→` with ● at 3 and shading to the left.
- For integer solutions, solve the inequality first, then list only the whole numbers that fit.

---

### ✏️ Worked Examples

**Example 1.** Solve 3(x − 2) ≥ 5x + 4 and show the solution on a number line.

3(x − 2) ≥ 5x + 4  
3x − 6 ≥ 5x + 4  
−6 ≥ 2x + 4  
−10 ≥ 2x  
−5 ≥ x, so **x ≤ −5**

Number line note: closed circle at −5, shaded to the left.

`←————●————→`  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;−5

---

**Example 2.** Solve −4 < 2x + 1 ≤ 9, then list the integer solutions.

−4 < 2x + 1 ≤ 9  
Subtract 1 from all three parts:  
−5 < 2x ≤ 8  
Divide all three parts by 2:  
−2.5 < x ≤ 4

Integer solutions: **−2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4**
