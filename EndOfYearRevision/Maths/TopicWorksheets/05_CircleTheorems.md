### 🔑 Key Ideas

1. **Angle at the centre is twice the angle at the circumference** — if both angles stand on the same arc/chord, the centre angle is double.
2. **Angles in the same segment are equal** — angles at the circumference standing on the same chord are equal.
3. **Angle in a semicircle = 90° (Thales)** — the angle made at the circumference by the ends of a diameter is a right angle.
4. **Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral sum to 180°** — if all four vertices are on a circle, opposite angles are supplementary.
5. **Tangent-radius theorem** — a tangent is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact.
6. **Two tangents from an external point are equal in length** — if two tangents are drawn from the same point, the tangent lengths are equal.
7. **Alternate Segment Theorem (stretch)** — the angle between a tangent and a chord equals the angle in the opposite segment.

---

### ✏️ Worked Examples

**Example 1.** A and B are points on a circle with centre O. Angle AOB = 110°. C is a point on the major arc AB, so angle ACB is the angle at the circumference subtended by chord AB. Find angle ACB.

**Solution:**  
Angle at the centre = 2 × angle at the circumference.  
So angle ACB = 110° ÷ 2 = **55°**.

---

**Example 2.** A, B, C and D are points on a circle, forming a cyclic quadrilateral in that order. Angle ABC = 68°. Find angle ADC.

**Solution:**  
Opposite angles in a cyclic quadrilateral sum to 180°.  
So angle ADC = 180° − 68° = **112°**.
