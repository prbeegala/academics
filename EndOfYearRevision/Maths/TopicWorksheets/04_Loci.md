### 🔑 Key Ideas

- **Fixed distance from a point:** the locus of points exactly r cm from a fixed point is a **circle** with radius r, centred on that point.
- **Fixed distance from a line segment:** the locus of points exactly r cm from a line segment is a **stadium shape**: two parallel lines r cm from the segment, joined by semicircles of radius r at each end.
- **Equidistant from two fixed points:** the locus is the **perpendicular bisector** of the line segment joining the two points.
- **Equidistant from two intersecting lines:** the locus is the **angle bisector** of the angle between the two lines.
- **Construct a perpendicular bisector:** draw equal-radius arcs from both endpoints of the segment; join the two arc intersections.
- **Construct an angle bisector:** draw an arc cutting both arms of the angle; from those two new points draw equal arcs; join the vertex to the arc intersection.
- **Construct a perpendicular from a point to a line:** from the point, draw equal arcs cutting the line in two places; construct the perpendicular bisector of those two points.
- **Construct an equilateral triangle:** draw a segment AB; with compass radius AB, draw arcs from A and B; join the arc intersection to A and B.
- For **region problems**, draw every boundary first, decide which side/inside/outside satisfies each condition, then shade only the overlap.

---

### ✏️ Worked Examples

**Example 1.** Describe the locus of points exactly 4 cm from a fixed point P.

**Answer:** A circle of radius 4 cm, centred on P.

**Example 2.** Construct the perpendicular bisector of AB where AB = 8 cm. Then describe the locus of points equidistant from A and B.

**Answer:** Draw AB = 8 cm. Set the compass to more than half of AB. Draw arcs above and below AB from A, then from B using the same radius. Join the two arc intersections. This line is the perpendicular bisector of AB, and it is the locus of all points equidistant from A and B.
