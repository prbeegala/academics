### 🔑 Key Ideas

- **Congruent shapes** are exactly the same shape and size. They may be rotated, reflected or translated.
- **Similar shapes** have the same shape but not necessarily the same size. Corresponding angles are equal and corresponding sides are in the same ratio.
- Triangle congruence rules:
  - **SSS:** three pairs of matching sides are equal.
  - **SAS:** two pairs of matching sides and the included angle are equal.
  - **ASA / AAS:** two pairs of matching angles and one matching side are equal.
  - **RHS:** right angle, hypotenuse and one other matching side are equal.
- For similar shapes:
  - **Linear scale factor** changes lengths.
  - **Area scale factor = (linear sf)²**.
  - **Volume scale factor = (linear sf)³**.
- Always match **corresponding** sides carefully before writing a ratio.

### ✏️ Worked Examples

**Example 1 — Congruence justification**  
Triangle ABC has AB = 5 cm, AC = 7 cm and angle A = 40°. Triangle DEF has DE = 5 cm, DF = 7 cm and angle D = 40°. Are the triangles congruent?

**Answer:** Yes. The equal angle is between the two equal sides in both triangles, so the triangles are congruent by **SAS**.

**Example 2 — Similar-shape ratio**  
Triangle PQR is similar to triangle XYZ. PQ corresponds to XY and QR corresponds to YZ. PQ = 6 cm, XY = 15 cm and QR = 8 cm. Find YZ.

**Answer:** Linear scale factor from PQR to XYZ = 15 ÷ 6 = 2.5.  
YZ = 8 × 2.5 = **20 cm**.
