### 🔑 Key Ideas

- Find two integers between which the root lies: one gives f(x) too low, and one gives f(x) too high.
- Try the midpoint or a 0.1-step trial.
- Decide whether the result is too high or too low, then halve the interval or narrow it down.
- To round to 1 d.p., test the midpoint of the final 0.1 interval; if too low ⇒ round up.

---

### ✏️ Worked Examples

**Example.** Solve x³ + 2x = 50 to 1 d.p.

Let f(x) = x³ + 2x. We want f(x) to be 50.

| x | f(x) | Comment |
|---|------|---------|
| 3 | 33.000 | too low |
| 4 | 72.000 | too high |
| 3.5 | 49.875 | too low |
| 3.6 | 53.856 | too high |
| 3.55 | 51.839 | too high |

The answer lies between 3.5 and 3.6.  
The midpoint is 3.55, and this is too high, so the value is closer to 3.5.

**Answer: x ≈ 3.5**
