# 📐 End-of-Year Maths Revision — Topic 04: Loci — Answers

| Q | Answer (brief working) |
|---|---|
| 1 | A circle of radius 5 cm centred on O. |
| 2 | A stadium shape: two lines parallel to AB, each 2 cm from AB, joined by semicircles of radius 2 cm centred at A and B. |
| 3 | The perpendicular bisector of AB: a straight line at 90° to AB through its midpoint. |
| 4 | The angle bisector: a straight line from V that halves the 80° angle into two 40° angles. |
| 5 | The constructed line is the perpendicular bisector of AB. It is the locus of points equidistant from A and B. |
| 6 | The angle bisector of angle ABC: a single straight line/ray from B making 35° with each arm. It is the locus of points equidistant from the two arms of the angle. |
| 7 | The perpendicular from P meets line l at 90°. This perpendicular segment is the shortest distance from P to line l. |
| 8 | A stadium shape around CD: two parallel line segments 1.5 cm from CD, joined by semicircles of radius 1.5 cm centred at C and D. |
| 9 | Draw the circle radius 3 cm centred at A. Draw the perpendicular bisector of AB. Shade the part inside the circle that is on A's side of the perpendicular bisector. Boundary: circular arc plus the relevant part of the perpendicular bisector. |
| 10 | Draw two lines parallel to l, each 2 cm from l, making a strip. Draw the circle radius 4 cm centred at T. Shade the overlap of the strip and the circle. Boundary: parts of the two parallel lines and the circle. |
| 11 | Draw the 60° angle and its angle bisector. Draw the circle radius 3 cm centred at M. Shade the part inside the angle and inside/on the circle that lies on the lower-road side of the angle bisector. The angle bisector boundary is included because equidistant is allowed. |
| 12 | From C the barn blocks a 90° corner, so the goat can graze a 270° sector of radius 5 m outside the barn. The rope cannot reach around the 8 m side, but it can reach the far corner along the 4 m side with 1 m left, giving an extra quarter-circle of radius 1 m around that adjacent corner. Area = 270/360 × π × 5² + 90/360 × π × 1² = 75π/4 + π/4 = 19π m². Boundary: a three-quarter circle of radius 5 m centred at C, plus a small quarter-circle of radius 1 m around the corner reached along the 4 m side. |
