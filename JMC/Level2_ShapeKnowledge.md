# Level 2 Shape Knowledge — Quick Reference Card

> **What is Level 2?** The "Shape Combinations" tier from the JMC Geometry study plan. These questions combine two or more known shapes and ask you to find a missing angle, perimeter, or area. They bridge Easy (Q1–8) and Medium (Q9–17) difficulty.

---

## Triangles

| Property | Rule |
|---|---|
| Angle sum | All angles add to **180°** |
| Equilateral | All angles = **60°**, all sides equal |
| Isosceles | **Base angles are equal** |
| Right-angled | One angle = 90°, other two add to 90° |

---

## Quadrilaterals

| Shape | Key angle properties |
|---|---|
| **Square** | All angles = **90°**, all sides equal |
| **Rectangle** | All angles = **90°** |
| **Parallelogram** | Opposite angles **equal**; consecutive angles **supplementary** (add to 180°) |
| **Rhombus** | Same as parallelogram + all sides equal; diagonals **bisect** the corner angles |
| **Kite** | One pair of opposite angles equal; diagonals meet at **90°** |
| **Trapezium** | Co-interior angles (same side) add to **180°** |

---

## Regular Polygons

| Shape | Sides | Interior angle | Exterior angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Equilateral triangle | 3 | 60° | 120° |
| Square | 4 | 90° | 90° |
| Pentagon | 5 | 108° | 72° |
| Hexagon | 6 | **120°** | 60° |
| Heptagon | 7 | ≈128.6° | ≈51.4° |
| Octagon | 8 | 135° | 45° |

**Formula:** Interior angle = **180° × (n − 2) ÷ n**

**Shortcut:** Exterior angles always add to **360°**, so each = **360° ÷ n**

## Lines & Angles

| Property | Rule |
|---|---|
| Angles on a straight line | Add to **180°** |
| Angles at a point | Add to **360°** |
| Vertically opposite angles | **Equal** |
| Parallel lines — alternate (Z) angles | **Equal** |
| Parallel lines — corresponding (F) angles | **Equal** |
| Parallel lines — co-interior (C) angles | Add to **180°** |

## JMC's Favourite Shape Combos

| Combo | What happens |
|---|---|
| Square + equilateral triangle (shared side) | Gap angle = 90° − 60° = **30°** or 90° + 60° = **150°** |
| Hexagon + square + equilateral triangle | Chain: 120° + 90° + 60° — find the leftover angles |
| Rhombus corner split by triangles | Use supplementary angles to find the corner, then subtract |
| Isosceles triangle with bisector | Creates two smaller triangles — chase equal base angles through both |

---

## The 3-Step Method

Works on almost every Level 2 question:

1. **Label what you know** — write interior angles of every named shape
2. **Find the big angle** — identify which corner is being split apart
3. **Subtract the pieces** — the answer is the leftover

### Worked Example: 2019 JMC Q8

> Kite PGRF inside rhombus PQRS. ∠PQG = 120°, ∠PSF = 120°, ∠PGQ = 35°, ∠PFS = 35°. Find ∠FPG.

1. **Label:** Rhombus consecutive angles are supplementary → ∠QPS = 180° − 120° = 60°
2. **Find pieces:** Triangle PQG → ∠GPQ = 180° − 120° − 35° = 25°. Triangle PSF → ∠FPS = 25°
3. **Subtract:** ∠FPG = 60° − 25° − 25° = **10°** ✓
