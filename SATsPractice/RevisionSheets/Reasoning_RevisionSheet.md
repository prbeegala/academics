# Maths Reasoning — Revision Sheet & Strategy Guide

The Reasoning papers test the same maths as Arithmetic, but **wrapped in words**. Most lost marks come from misreading, not miscalculating. This sheet covers the strategies and the topics most often tested.

---

## A. Five golden rules for Reasoning papers

1. **Read the question twice.** Underline what is being asked. Many Year 6 children answer the wrong question.
2. **Highlight the numbers and the units.** Watch for swaps — *minutes ↔ hours*, *grams ↔ kilograms*, *cm ↔ m*.
3. **Show your working** for any 2-mark question — method marks are awarded even if the final answer is wrong.
4. **Estimate first.** Roughly, what should the answer be? If you get something wildly different, you've slipped.
5. **Final check:** does the answer make sense in the real world? *(e.g. a car can't travel 600 mph; a child can't be 200 cm tall on average.)*

## B. Topics most likely to appear

### 1. Place value & rounding
- Rounding: look at the **next digit**. 5 or more rounds up; less than 5 rounds down.
- 47 386 → nearest 10 = 47 390; nearest 100 = 47 400; nearest 1000 = 47 000.

### 2. Negative numbers
- Going from −3 °C to 24 °C = **27 degrees** (count up through zero: 3 + 24).
- Subtracting a negative = adding. *5 − (−3) = 8.*

### 3. Fractions, decimals, percentages
- **Equivalents to memorise:**
  - 1/2 = 0.5 = 50%
  - 1/4 = 0.25 = 25%
  - 3/4 = 0.75 = 75%
  - 1/5 = 0.2 = 20%
  - 1/10 = 0.1 = 10%
  - 1/3 ≈ 0.33 ≈ 33%
  - 1/8 = 0.125 = 12.5%
- **Adding fractions:** make denominators match first.
- **Of:** *3/4 of 240* = 240 ÷ 4 × 3 = 180.
- **Percentages:** *15% of £40* = 10% (£4) + 5% (£2) = **£6**. Build from 10%, 5%, 1%.

### 4. Ratio
- "**Ratio 3 : 4**, total 21" → 3 + 4 = **7 parts**, each part = 21 ÷ 7 = 3, so 9 and 12.
- **Recipe scaling:** find amount per 1 unit, then multiply.

### 5. Algebra
- *3x + 5 = 26* → subtract 5 (3x = 21) → divide by 3 (x = 7).
- *2(y − 5) = 18* → expand or divide first: y − 5 = 9 → y = 14.

### 6. Geometry
- Angles in a triangle = **180°**. In a quadrilateral = **360°**. On a straight line = **180°**. Around a point = **360°**.
- Perimeter = total length around. Area of rectangle = L × W. Area of triangle = (base × height) ÷ 2.
- Volume of cuboid = L × W × H.
- A regular polygon: all sides and angles equal.
- Lines of symmetry: square = 4, rectangle = 2, equilateral triangle = 3, regular hexagon = 6.

### 7. Coordinates
- Always go **along then up** — (x, y).
- Midpoint of (a,b) and (c,d) = ((a+c)/2 , (b+d)/2).

### 8. Time
- Convert carefully: *14:38 → 19:12* = 14:38 → 19:38 (5 hours) − 26 minutes = **4 h 34 min.**
- Or: 14:38 → 15:00 (22 min) → 19:00 (4 h) → 19:12 (12 min) = 4 h 34 min.

### 9. Measure conversions
- 1 km = 1000 m;  1 m = 100 cm;  1 cm = 10 mm
- 1 kg = 1000 g;  1 litre = 1000 ml
- ÷ to go to bigger unit; × to go to smaller unit.

### 10. Statistics
- **Mean** = total ÷ how many. To find a missing value: (mean × count) − (sum of known) = missing.
- **Mode** = most common.  **Median** = middle when ordered.  **Range** = highest − lowest.
- **Pie charts:** 360° represents the whole. 90° = 1/4 of total.

### 11. Probability
- Total of all probabilities = **1**. P(not A) = 1 − P(A).

### 12. BIDMAS / Order of operations
**B**rackets → **I**ndices (powers) → **D**ivision/**M**ultiplication → **A**ddition/**S**ubtraction.
> 12 − (3 + 4) × 2 = 12 − 14 = **−2** (NOT 5×2 = 10 then 12−10).

## C. Speed-up tricks

- **× 5:** halve and × 10. *36 × 5 = 360 ÷ 2 = 180.*
- **× 9:** × 10 then subtract one lot. *27 × 9 = 270 − 27 = 243.*
- **÷ 5:** double and ÷ 10. *85 ÷ 5 = 170 ÷ 10 = 17.*
- **× 25:** × 100 ÷ 4. *36 × 25 = 3600 ÷ 4 = 900.*
- **% by halving:** 10% → 5% → 2.5% → 1.25%, etc.

---

## D. Common-mistake checklist

- [ ] Did I copy the numbers down correctly?
- [ ] Did I notice the **units** the question wants?
- [ ] If money: have I put the **decimal point** in (£3.50 not £3.5)?
- [ ] Did I answer in the right form (fraction in simplest form? mixed number? percentage?)?
- [ ] For multi-step problems, am I sure I did **all** the steps?
- [ ] Did I include a **label** if the question asks for the units (cm, kg, °)?

## E. 60-second mental warm-ups (to do daily)

1. Count up in 0.25s from 0 to 5.
2. Recite squares 1²–12² and cubes 1³–5³.
3. Halve and double random numbers (e.g. half of 86? double 47?).
4. Do a quick "fraction flash": 3/8 + 1/4 = ?  2/3 of 21 = ?
5. Time conversions: how many minutes is 2¼ hours? (135)
