# Quick-Reference Flashcards

Cut along the lines, fold, and self-test. Or just cover the right column and try.

## SPAG flashcards

| Prompt | Answer |
|--------|--------|
| What is a fronted adverbial? | A word/phrase at the start of a sentence describing how/when/where, followed by a comma. *Yesterday, she left.* |
| What is a relative clause? | A clause beginning with *who/which/that/whose/where* that adds info about a noun. |
| What is a subordinate clause? | A clause that cannot stand alone, joined by a subordinating conjunction (because, although, when…). |
| Difference between *its* and *it's* | *its* = belonging to it; *it's* = it is. |
| When do you use a semi-colon? | To join two **independent** clauses that are closely related. |
| When do you use a colon? | To introduce a list or an explanation. |
| Active or passive: *The cake was eaten by the boy.* | Passive |
| Word class of *quickly*? | Adverb |
| Word class of *those*? | Determiner |
| Subjunctive form of "If I was you"? | "If I **were** you" |
| Formal version of *kids*? | children |
| Past perfect of "I run"? | I had run |
| What punctuation surrounds parenthesis? | Commas, brackets, or dashes (a matching pair). |
