# SPAG Revision Sheet (Quick Reference)

A focused crib sheet for the trickiest SPAG topics. Read through twice; cover and self-test.

---

## 1. Word classes (always ask: *what is this word doing?*)
| Class | Job | Example |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Noun | names a person/place/thing | *cat, school, freedom* |
| Pronoun | replaces a noun | *she, them, mine* |
| Verb | action / state of being | *runs, was, seems* |
| Adjective | describes a noun | *bright, careful* |
| Adverb | describes a verb / adj / other adv | *quickly, very, soon* |
| Preposition | shows position/time/direction | *under, before, into* |
| Conjunction | joins clauses/words | *because, but, although* |
| Determiner | introduces a noun | *the, a, those, my, three* |

> **Trap:** *fast* can be an adjective (*a fast car*) **or** an adverb (*she ran fast*). Look at what it's modifying.

---

## 2. Sentence structures
- **Simple:** one main clause. *Srita revised.*
- **Compound:** two main clauses joined by **FANBOYS** (for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so). *Srita revised, **but** her brother played.*
- **Complex:** main clause + subordinate clause introduced by a **subordinating conjunction** (because, although, while, when, if, since, until, before, after, even though). *Although it was late, Srita revised.*

> **Tip:** A subordinate clause **cannot** stand alone as a sentence.

---

## 3. Punctuation power-list
| Mark | When to use | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| `,` | between items in a list, after a fronted adverbial, around parenthesis, before a conjunction joining clauses | *After lunch, we played.* / *Mr Khan, our teacher, smiled.* |
| `;` | joins two **closely-related** main clauses (each could stand alone) | *I love maths; my brother loves art.* |
| `:` | introduces a list **or** an explanation | *She packed three things: a pen, a book, a snack.* |
| `–` (dash) | adds emphasis or interruption (informal) | *She paused — and then ran.* |
| `( )` | parenthesis (extra info that can be removed) | *Srita (aged 11) won the prize.* |
| `'` | possession **or** contraction | *James's bag* / *don't* |
| `"..."` | direct speech | *"Hello," said Srita.* |

### Direct speech rules (often tested!)
1. Speech goes inside inverted commas.
2. Punctuation **inside** the closing inverted comma.
3. New speaker = new line.
4. Capital letter to start the speech.

> *"Where are you going?" asked Mum.*
> *"To school," replied Srita.*

---

## 4. Apostrophes (most-marked-wrong topic!)
- **Possession singular:** add `'s` → *the dog's bone, James's coat.*
- **Possession plural ending in s:** add `'` only → *the dogs' bones, the boys' bags.*
- **Possession plural NOT ending in s:** add `'s` → *the children's coats.*
- **Contraction:** apostrophe replaces missing letters → *don't (do not), it's (it is), they're (they are).*
- **its** = belonging to it (NO apostrophe). **it's** = it is.

---

## 5. Tenses
| Tense | Form | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| Present simple | base verb | *She walks.* |
| Present progressive | is/are + -ing | *She is walking.* |
| Past simple | -ed / irregular | *She walked.* |
| Past progressive | was/were + -ing | *She was walking.* |
| Present perfect | has/have + past participle | *She has walked.* |
| Past perfect | had + past participle | *She had walked.* |
| Future simple | will + verb | *She will walk.* |
| Future perfect | will have + past participle | *She will have walked.* |

---

## 6. Active vs Passive
- **Active:** Subject does the action. *The dog chased the cat.*
- **Passive:** Subject receives the action. *The cat was chased by the dog.*

> Switch test: in passive, you can usually add "by …" at the end.

---

## 7. Subjunctive (rare but tested!)
Use **were** (not *was*) for hypotheticals:
- *If I **were** taller, I would reach.*
- *I wish she **were** here.*

Also: *The teacher requested that he **be** quiet.*

---

## 8. Formal vs Informal
| Informal | Formal |
|----------|--------|
| *I think* | *I believe / In my opinion* |
| *get* | *receive / obtain* |
| *kids* | *children* |
| *gonna / wanna* | *going to / want to* |
| *a lot of* | *many / a great deal of* |
| Contractions (*don't*) | Full forms (*do not*) |

---

## 9. Top traps Srita should watch for
1. **fewer vs less** — *fewer* for countable (fewer apples), *less* for uncountable (less water).
2. **affect vs effect** — *affect* (verb), *effect* (noun, usually).
3. **their / there / they're** — possession / place / they are.
4. **Tick ONE box** vs **Tick all that apply** — read carefully!
5. **Underline** vs **circle** vs **tick** — follow exactly what's asked.
6. **Relative clause** = adds info using *who / which / that / whose / where*.
7. **Fronted adverbial** needs a comma after it. *Yesterday morning, she left early.*
