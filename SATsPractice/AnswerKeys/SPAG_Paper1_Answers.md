# Answer Key — SPAG Paper 1 (Grammar & Punctuation)

1. **Although** (subordinating conjunction)
2. *By the time the bell rang, she had finished her work.* (past perfect)
3. *The headteacher**,** who is retiring next year**,** gave a moving speech.*
4. *The cat was chased across the garden by the dog.* (passive)
5. Srita said (that) she would visit Godolphin the next day. *(or: the following day)*
6. *The book **that I borrowed from the library** is on the desk.*
7. *The **children's** coats were hanging on the pegs.*
8. **Adverb**
9. *I love maths; English is my second favourite.* (semi-colon joining two main clauses)
10. **might**
11. *If I **were** you, I would revise more.*
12. *Srita packed three things**:** her pencil case, her water bottle and her lunch.*
13. *"Where are you going?" asked the teacher.*
14. **the small grey cat** (subject of the sentence)
15. *excellent / outstanding / impressive* (any formal synonym)
16. **Those, three, the** (all are determiners)
17. *She will have finished the book by Friday.* (future perfect)
18. *She is a **well-known** author.*
19. *Even though it was late, **she kept reading her book**.* (main clause)
20. **over** (preposition)
21. *Mrs Patel **(my form teacher)** organised the trip.*
22. *Close the door quietly.* (command — imperative)
23. *I wasn't sure what to do… so I waited.* (single-character ellipsis)
24. *The sun is rising over the field.* (statement)
25. **quietly** and **slowly**
26. *"Run as fast as you can!" shouted the coach.*
27. *After the match, the team celebrated loudly.* (or another fronted adverbial)
28. *The fluffy ginger cat with bright green eyes slept.*
29. **a birthday card** (direct object). *(Acceptable: "her friend" as indirect object — but "what was given?" = card.)*
30. *She thought about it for ages **—** and then she finally decided.*
31. **hers** (possessive pronoun) — *mine* is also a possessive pronoun, accept either or both circled
32. *The boy broke the window.*
33. *The dog barked because it heard a noise.*
34. *The tall, mysterious stranger* (noun phrase)
35. *Let's eat**,** Grandma.*
36. **Past perfect progressive** (or *past perfect continuous*)
37. *There were **fewer** people at the party.*
38. *I think the trip will be excellent.*
39. *The two **girls'** bags were left in **James's** locker.* (also accept *James'*)
40. **mean**
41. *…aren't you?*
42. *The book **that I read** was excellent.*
43. Prefix: **un-**  Meaning: *not / opposite of*
44. Suffix: **-ship**  Indicates: *a state or condition (of being a friend)*
45. *Srita loves maths**;** her brother loves science.*
46. **Monday, Srita, Castleview Primary School, Mrs Khan** (4 proper nouns — 2 marks for all/most)
47. *She is walking home.* (present progressive)
48. *She bought the following items**:** pens, pencils and a ruler.*
49. *<u>I went to the park</u> **(although)** <u>it was raining heavily</u>.*
50. *My grandma**, who is 80 years old,** runs every morning.*  *(or: My grandma, who runs every morning, is 80 years old.)*

**Total: 50 marks**

### Score guide
- 45–50: Excellent — distinction level. Polish only.
- 38–44: Strong — review the questions you missed; the topic types will tell you what to focus on.
- 30–37: Solid base — re-read the SPAG Revision Sheet, focusing on apostrophes, clauses and tenses.
- Under 30: Slow down and re-attempt — read each question twice; underline command words ("tick", "circle", "rewrite").
