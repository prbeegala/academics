# Answer Key — Reading Non-Fiction Practice (*The Surprising Genius of the Octopus*)

1. **Octopuses** (1)

2. **bright** *(also accept "cleverest" or "intelligent" if Srita circles those — "bright" is the closest single-word synonym for "clever".)* (1)

3. **About the size of a walnut.** (1)

4. The octopus doesn't only think with its brain — its **arms also contain nerve cells**, so each arm can **sense and decide things on its own**, while the central brain handles the bigger choices. (2)
   *(1 mark for "the arms can think/decide" idea; 1 mark for "the brain coordinates / works together with the arms".)*

5. Because Otto kept **squirting water at the overhead lamp** to turn the lights off, and this short-circuited the building. They realised they couldn't stop him, so they let him have the dark he wanted. (1)

6. **Chromatophores** are **special cells in an octopus's skin**. (1) They **allow it to change colour in less than a second** (and help it camouflage). (1)

7. It can **impersonate the shape and movement of at least fifteen other sea creatures**, such as flatfish or sea snakes — not just change colour or texture. (1)

8. Because an octopus has to **learn everything extremely quickly**, in just one or two years. (1) It also has to learn **without ever meeting its parents or being taught**, which is unusual and impressive. (1)

9. It means an octopus's intelligence has evolved **completely separately from ours** — our last common ancestor was 600 million years ago and had no real brain. (1) So when we meet an octopus, we are meeting a mind that is **as different from ours as an alien's might be**, even though it lives on the same planet. (1)

10. **To inform and entertain the reader about how clever octopuses are.** (1)

11. (2) Award 1 mark for two correct facts; 1 mark for a correct opinion.
    - **Facts** (any two of): octopuses have brains the size of a walnut; they have nerve cells in each arm; chromatophores let them change colour in under a second; the mimic octopus lives in Indonesia; most octopuses live only 1–2 years; our last common ancestor was around 600 million years ago.
    - **Opinions** (any one): "the common octopus is one of the most intelligent animals in the sea"; "and certainly the most surprising"; "Perhaps the strangest thing of all is how different an octopus is from us."

**Total: 15 marks**

### Marking guidance
- For inference questions (4, 8, 9), reward **clear meaning** even if expressed simply.
- For Q11, the **opinion** must be a judgement ("most intelligent", "most surprising", "strangest") — not a verifiable fact.
