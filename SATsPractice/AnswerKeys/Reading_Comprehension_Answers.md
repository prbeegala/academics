# Answer Key — Reading Comprehension (*The Lighthouse Keeper's Secret*)

1. **At the top of the iron staircase / in the lamp room of the Hawksmoor lighthouse.** (1)
2. **echoing** *(also accept "empty")* (1)
3. **The Devil's Teeth** (1)
4. Any **two** of: (2)
   - A storm is rolling in from the west.
   - A fishing fleet is due back at any moment.
   - The light has failed for the first time anyone can remember.
5. The lighthouse light is a commitment to keep sailors safe. Sailors **rely on / trust** the lamp to guide them away from danger; the keeper is responsible for protecting their lives. (2)
6. **"the rain struck the windows like gunfire"** (1)
7. **determined** (1)
8. **As a backup in case the first one ever failed** — so the promise to sailors could still be kept. (1)
9. The grandfather is shown as: (3)
   - **Wise / experienced:** taught Mira about tides, birds, and the lamp.
   - **Caring / loving:** spent summers teaching her, addressed her as "my brave one".
   - **Dutiful / responsible:** prepared a hidden second mechanism for emergencies.
   - **Trusting in Mira:** left her the key and instructions, knowing she would come.
   *(Award marks for any two valid points + textual evidence.)*
10. It shows Mira is **calm under pressure, brave, and dedicated**. The "small smile" suggests pride in her grandfather and quiet confidence; "set to work" shows she puts duty before her own feelings. (2)
11. Order: (2)
    - **2** Mira finds a key under a floorboard.
    - **1** Mira climbs the iron staircase.
    - *(actually note that climbing the staircase is event 1; reading the note is 2; rain hitting windows is 3; key is 4; cabinet is 5)* — **Correct order:**
    - 1 — climbs the iron staircase
    - 2 — reads a note in her grandfather's handwriting
    - 3 — the first lash of rain hits the windows
    - 4 — finds a key under a floorboard
    - 5 — opens the cabinet behind the workbench
12. They create a **threatening, dangerous atmosphere**. "From the west" suggests an oncoming threat; "like gunfire" makes the rain sound violent and sudden, building tension and danger. (2)
13. Possible techniques (any **two** with explanation): (3)
    - **Weather imagery** ("wind howled", "rain like gunfire") — sets a stormy, urgent backdrop.
    - **Short, punchy sentences** ("The handwriting was unmistakably her grandfather's.") — quicken pace.
    - **Mystery / withheld information** — the failed light, the hidden note, the locked cabinet — make the reader want to know more.
    - **Sensory detail** (echoing footsteps, brass gleaming, thudding heart) — pull the reader into Mira's experience.
    - **Time pressure** — the fleet is due, the storm is coming.
14. The promise is the **lighthouse keeper's promise to keep the lamp burning so sailors can find their way home safely**. It is important to Mira because it links her to her grandfather's legacy and the lives of the fishing fleet depend on it; keeping the promise honours her training and her family's duty. (3)
