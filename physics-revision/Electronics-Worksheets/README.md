## 📚 Where to start when stuck

Read **[`../Electronics-Revision.md`](../Electronics-Revision.md)** first — it has all the rules and worked examples in one place. Use it as your reference while you practise.

## 🎯 How to revise

1. **Read** the matching section of `Electronics-Revision.md`.
2. **Work through** the worksheet in pencil, showing every step.
3. **Mark yourself** against the `_Answers.md` mark scheme — give yourself part-marks for correct method even if the final number is wrong.
4. Note any rule you slipped up on and re-read that section of the revision notes.
5. When all four topic worksheets feel solid, sit the **mock test** under exam conditions: 45 minutes, no notes, closed book.

## ✅ Quick checklist before the real exam

- I can recognise the symbols for: cell, battery, switch, lamp, resistor, variable resistor, ammeter, voltmeter, diode, LED, motor, fuse, LDR.
- I know the units: amps (A), volts (V), ohms (Ω).
- Ammeter goes in **series**; voltmeter goes in **parallel**.
- Series: current the same, voltage shares, resistance adds, one break stops everything.
- Parallel: voltage the same on each branch, currents add to the total, branches independent.
- I can use **V = I × R** to find any one of V, I or R from the other two.
- I always include units in my answers.
