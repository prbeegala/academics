# 🔑 Answer Key — 03 Series Circuits

| Q | Answer (with working) |
|---|------------------------|
| 1 | **0.4 A**. Current is the **same everywhere** in a series circuit, so an ammeter between A and B reads the same value as before. |
| 2 | (a) I = V ÷ R = 6 ÷ 3 = **2 A**. (b) The resistor is the only component, so it takes the **whole 6 V**. (V = I × R = 2 × 3 = 6 V ✓.) |
| 3 | (a) R_total = 4 + 6 = **10 Ω**. (b) I = V ÷ R = 10 ÷ 10 = **1 A**. |
| 4 | (a) R_total = 2 + 4 = **6 Ω**. (b) I = 12 ÷ 6 = **2 A**. (c) V across 2 Ω = I × R = 2 × 2 = **4 V**. (d) V across 4 Ω = 2 × 4 = **8 V**. (e) 4 + 8 = **12 V** ✓ — matches the battery. |
| 5 | The **other two lamps also go out**. Removing one lamp **breaks the single loop** — there is no longer a complete circuit, so no current can flow anywhere. |
| 6 | (a) Total resistance **increases** (doubles, if the bulbs are identical). (b) Current **decreases** (halves, if resistance doubles, since I = V/R). (c) Each bulb is **dimmer** (less current and a smaller share of the voltage). |
| 7 | 1.5 + 1.5 = **3.0 V**. Cells in series add up. |
| 8 | (a) Voltage shares equally between three identical bulbs: 4.5 ÷ 3 = **1.5 V** each. (b) 9 ÷ 3 = **3.0 V** each — the bulbs would be much brighter (and might burn out if rated for less). |
| 9 | (a) R_total = 3 + 9 = **12 Ω**. (b) I = 12 ÷ 12 = **1 A**. (c) V across 9 Ω = 1 × 9 = **9 V**. (d) The **9 Ω resistor** gets the bigger share, because in series a **larger resistance** takes a **larger share** of the voltage (same current × bigger R → bigger V). |
| 10 | (a) R = V ÷ I = 2 ÷ 0.5 = **4 Ω**. (b) **0.5 A** — same as the ammeter reading; current is identical everywhere in a series circuit. |
| 11 | Christmas lights in series form **one single loop**, so the current has only **one path**. If even one bulb's filament breaks, the loop is broken and **no current can flow** in the loop, so **all the other bulbs go out** too. |
| 12 | (a) V_R₂ = V_total − V_R₁ = 6 − 2 = **4 V**. (b) R₁ = V ÷ I = 2 ÷ 0.5 = **4 Ω**. (c) R₂ = V ÷ I = 4 ÷ 0.5 = **8 Ω**. (d) R_total = 4 + 8 = **12 Ω**. (Check: I = V/R_total = 6/12 = 0.5 A ✓.) |

### Marking tips
- Mark working as well as final answer — partial credit for correct method.
- Insist on **units** (A, V, Ω). One mark off for missing units in calculation questions.
- For "what happens" questions, look for both the **outcome** ("dimmer / both go out") *and* the **reason** ("voltage shared / loop broken"). Award one mark for each.

### Common slip-ups
- Mixing up V_total and V across a single component.
- Forgetting that in series, current is the *same*, while voltage *shares*.
- Adding resistances in parallel — that's a different rule! In **series** they simply add.
- Treating "battery dies if one bulb removed" as wrong — it isn't; with no current flowing the cell isn't being drained, but in the question's terms "everything stops" is correct.
