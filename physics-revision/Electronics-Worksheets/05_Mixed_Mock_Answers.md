## Section C — Calculations (12 marks)

**Q9.** Series, V = 12 V, R₁ = 2 Ω, R₂ = 4 Ω. [5 marks]
a) R_total = 2 + 4 = **6 Ω**. [1]
b) I = V ÷ R = 12 ÷ 6 = **2 A**. [1]
c) V₁ = I × R₁ = 2 × 2 = **4 V**. [1]
d) V₂ = I × R₂ = 2 × 4 = **8 V**. [1]
e) 4 + 8 = **12 V ✓**. [1]

**Q10.** Parallel, V = 12 V, R₁ = 2 Ω, R₂ = 4 Ω. [5 marks]
a) V across R₁ = **12 V**. [1]
b) V across R₂ = **12 V**. [1]
c) I₁ = 12 ÷ 2 = **6 A**. [1]
d) I₂ = 12 ÷ 4 = **3 A**. [1]
e) I_total = 6 + 3 = **9 A**. [1]

**Q11.** [2 marks]
- The **parallel** arrangement has the larger total current (9 A vs 2 A in series). [1]
- Reason (any of): in parallel each branch gets the full voltage so each draws its own current and they add up; / the parallel total resistance (≈ 1.33 Ω) is much lower than the series total (6 Ω), and lower R with the same V means bigger I. [1]

a) Diagram [3]:
- 1 mark: battery, switch and ammeter all in the **main wire** (in series with each other), before the split.

- 1 mark: closed circuit, correct symbols, ruler-straight wires.

```
     +    −
   ─┤├──/──(A)──┬──────┐
   │            │      │
   │           (×)    [ ]    ← 3 Ω lamp ; 6 Ω resistor
   │           3Ω      6Ω
   │            │      │
   └────────────┴──────┘
```

b) Branch 1: I₁ = V ÷ R = 6 ÷ 3 = **2 A**. [1]
c) Branch 2: I₂ = V ÷ R = 6 ÷ 6 = **1 A**. [1]
d) Total current = I₁ + I₂ = 2 + 1 = **3 A**. [1]
