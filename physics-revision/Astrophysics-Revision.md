## 🌍 1. The Earth

### Day and night — the spin of the Earth on its axis
- The Earth **spins on its own axis** once every **24 hours**.
- The axis is an imaginary line running through the **North and South Poles**.
- As the Earth spins, the side facing the Sun has **day**, and the side facing away has **night**.
- The Sun isn't really "moving" across the sky — *we* are turning. The Sun appears to rise in the east and set in the west because Earth rotates from west to east.

### The origin of the seasons
- The Earth's axis is **tilted at about 23.5°** compared to its orbit around the Sun.
- The Earth takes **365¼ days (1 year)** to orbit the Sun.
- Because of the tilt:
  - When the **Northern Hemisphere tilts towards the Sun**, the UK has **summer** — longer days, more direct sunlight, warmer.
  - When the **Northern Hemisphere tilts away from the Sun**, the UK has **winter** — shorter days, sunlight hits at a shallow angle, colder.
  - Spring and autumn happen in between when neither hemisphere is tilted strongly toward the Sun.
- ⚠️ Common mistake: seasons are **NOT** caused by Earth being closer or further from the Sun. The orbit is almost circular. It's the **tilt** that matters.

## 🌙 2. The Moon and Eclipses

### Phases of the Moon
The Moon doesn't make its own light — it **reflects** sunlight. As it orbits Earth (every ~27 days), we see different amounts of the lit half:

| Phase | What you see |
|---|---|
| **New Moon** | Moon is between Earth and Sun — lit side faces away, we see nothing. |
| **Crescent** | A thin sliver — less than half lit. |
| **First/Third Quarter** | Half the Moon is lit (a "half moon"). |
| **Gibbous** | More than half lit, but not full. |
| **Full Moon** | Earth is between Sun and Moon — we see the whole lit side. |
| **Waxing** | Lit area is **growing** (new → full). |
| **Waning** | Lit area is **shrinking** (full → new). |

### The Moon's spin period = its orbital period
- The Moon takes the **same time to spin once on its axis as it does to orbit the Earth** (~27 days).
- This is called being **tidally locked**.
- That's why we **always see the same face** of the Moon from Earth — the "far side" never points toward us.

### Why the Moon and Sun look the same size
- The Sun is **about 400 times wider** than the Moon.
- But the Sun is **about 400 times further away** than the Moon.
- These two ratios cancel out, so they appear roughly the **same size in the sky** — a happy coincidence that makes total solar eclipses possible.

### Solar and lunar eclipses

**Solar eclipse** — Moon blocks the Sun.
- Order: **Sun → Moon → Earth**.
- Happens at **new Moon** (rarely — only when alignment is exact).
- The Moon's shadow falls on Earth; daytime briefly goes dark.

**Lunar eclipse** — Earth blocks sunlight from reaching the Moon.
- Order: **Sun → Earth → Moon**.
- Happens at **full Moon**.
- Earth's shadow falls on the Moon, often making it look reddish ("blood Moon").

## ⚖️ 3. Weight, Mass and Gravity

### Mass vs Weight
| | Mass | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| What it is | Amount of matter in an object | Force of gravity acting on the object |
| Unit | kilograms (kg) | newtons (N) |
| Changes with location? | **No** — same on Earth, Moon, in space | **Yes** — depends on gravitational field strength |
| Type of quantity | Scalar | Vector (force) |

### Equation: weight = mass × gravitational field strength
$$W = m \times g$$
- **W** = weight (N)
- **m** = mass (kg)
- **g** = gravitational field strength (N/kg)
  - On **Earth**: g ≈ **10 N/kg** (sometimes 9.8)
  - On the **Moon**: g ≈ 1.6 N/kg
  - On **Jupiter**: g ≈ 25 N/kg

**Example:** A 5 kg bag on Earth → W = 5 × 10 = **50 N**. On the Moon → W = 5 × 1.6 = **8 N**. Mass stays 5 kg in both places.

### Orbiting is "falling freely under gravity"
- An object in orbit is constantly being **pulled towards** the planet by gravity.
- It's also moving **sideways** very fast.
- The result: it keeps falling toward the planet but keeps **missing it**, so it travels in a curved path — an orbit.
- Astronauts on the ISS feel "weightless" because they (and the station) are **falling around Earth together**.

### Newton's cannon — explaining orbits
- Imagine firing a cannonball horizontally from a very tall mountain.
- Slow shot → falls to ground nearby.
- Faster shot → travels further before hitting ground.
- Fast enough → the ground curves away beneath it as fast as it falls — it never lands. It's in **orbit**!
- This thought experiment shows orbiting is just falling at the right speed.

### Equation: orbital speed
$$v = \frac{2 \pi r}{T}$$
- **v** = orbital speed (m/s)
- **r** = orbital radius (m) — distance from centre of the body it orbits
- **T** = orbital time period (s) — time for one full orbit
- This is just "circumference of orbit ÷ time taken".

**Example:** A satellite orbits at r = 7,000,000 m, T = 6,000 s.
v = (2 × π × 7,000,000) / 6,000 ≈ **7,330 m/s**.

## 🌌 4. What's in the Solar System and Beyond

### Naked-eye objects
Objects you can see without a telescope:
- **The Sun** (a star)
- **The Moon**
- **Planets**: Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn (all visible to the naked eye)
- **Stars**
- **Comets** — icy bodies with glowing tails when near the Sun
- **Meteors** — "shooting stars"; small rocks burning up in Earth's atmosphere

### Natural vs artificial satellites
- **Natural satellite**: a naturally occurring object orbiting a planet. The **Moon** is Earth's natural satellite.
- **Artificial satellite**: human-made object launched into orbit (e.g. the ISS, GPS satellites, communications satellites).

### Planets in order from the Sun
**My Very Easy Method Just Speeds Up Names**:
1. **M**ercury
2. **V**enus
3. **E**arth
4. **M**ars
5. **J**upiter
6. **S**aturn
7. **U**ranus
8. **N**eptune

### Orbits are ellipses
- Planets don't orbit in perfect circles — orbits are **ellipses** (slightly squashed circles).
- This was discovered by **Johannes Kepler**.

### Inner (terrestrial) vs outer (gas giant) planets
| Inner / Terrestrial | Outer / Gas giants |
|---|---|
| Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars | Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune |
| Small, rocky | Huge, made mostly of gas (hydrogen, helium) |
| Few or no moons | Many moons + rings |
| Closer to the Sun, hotter | Far from the Sun, very cold |
| Higher density | Lower density |

The **asteroid belt** (between Mars and Jupiter) separates the inner and outer planets.

### Planets vs dwarf planets
- A **planet** must: (1) orbit the Sun, (2) be roughly spherical, (3) have **cleared its orbit** of other debris.
- A **dwarf planet** meets the first two but **hasn't cleared its orbit** — examples: **Pluto**, Eris, Ceres, Makemake, Haumea.

### The asteroid belt
- A region of rocky debris **between Mars and Jupiter**.
- Made of leftover material from when the solar system formed — Jupiter's gravity prevented it from forming a planet.

### The Kuiper Belt and Oort Cloud
- **Kuiper Belt**: a doughnut-shaped region beyond Neptune containing icy bodies (Pluto lives here). Source of short-period comets.
- **Oort Cloud**: a much further, spherical shell of icy objects surrounding the entire solar system. Source of long-period comets.

### Stars, galaxies and the universe
- **Almost every star** you see at night is part of our galaxy, the **Milky Way**.
- A **galaxy** is a huge, gravitationally-bound group of **billions of stars**, plus gas, dust and dark matter.
- The **Milky Way** contains about **100 billion stars**.
- The nearest large galaxy to us is **Andromeda**.
- There are around **100 billion galaxies** in the observable universe.

### The geocentric model
- Believed Earth was at the centre, with everything orbiting it.
- Promoted by **Ptolemy** (~150 CE).
- Used **epicycles** (loops within loops) to explain why planets sometimes appear to move backwards.

**Why was it accepted for so long (~1,500 years)?**
1. It matches what we *see* — Sun and stars appear to move around us.
2. It made reasonably accurate predictions for calendars and eclipses.
3. It fit religious and philosophical beliefs that humans/Earth were special.
4. **Aristotle's** authority went almost unquestioned in Europe.
5. There were no telescopes or precise instruments to disprove it.
6. Challenging the Church was dangerous (Galileo was put on trial).

### The heliocentric model
- The **Sun is at the centre**; planets (including Earth) orbit it.
- Proposed by **Copernicus (1543)**.
- Supported by **Galileo's** telescope observations:
  - Moons orbiting Jupiter (proving not everything orbits Earth).
  - Phases of Venus (only possible if Venus orbits the Sun).
- **Kepler** showed orbits are ellipses; **Newton** explained orbits with gravity.

### The scientific process
- Theories are accepted if they explain the available evidence.
- **Better tools → better evidence → better models.**
- Scientists are willing to **change their minds** when new evidence appears, even if it overturns long-held ideas (geocentric → heliocentric is the classic example).

## 🌈 5. The Electromagnetic (EM) Spectrum

### Order of the EM spectrum
From **longest wavelength / lowest frequency / lowest energy** → **shortest wavelength / highest frequency / highest energy**:

**Radio → Microwaves → Infrared → Visible light → Ultraviolet → X-rays → Gamma rays**

Memory aid: **R**aging **M**artians **I**nvaded **V**enus **U**sing **X**-ray **G**uns.

### Light, EM radiation, EM waves
- These three terms mean the **same thing** — they all describe waves in the EM spectrum.
- "Light" is often used for the visible part, but technically all EM waves are "light".

### Direction of increasing frequency, wavelength and energy

| Going from radio → gamma | Goes |
|---|---|
| Frequency | **Increases** ⬆ |
| Energy | **Increases** ⬆ |
| Wavelength | **Decreases** ⬇ |

(Higher frequency = more energy = shorter wavelength.)

### Speed of light
- All EM waves travel at the **speed of light in a vacuum**:
  $$c = 300{,}000{,}000 \text{ m/s} = 3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$$
- This is the **fastest speed possible** in the universe.

### Light-time calculations
Use: **distance = speed × time**, so **time = distance ÷ speed**.

**Example: How long does light take to travel from the Sun to the Earth?**
- Distance: 150,000,000,000 m (1.5 × 10¹¹ m)
- t = (1.5 × 10¹¹) ÷ (3 × 10⁸) = **500 seconds ≈ 8 minutes 20 seconds**

So sunlight you see now actually left the Sun about **8 minutes ago**!

## 💥 6. The Big Bang Theory

### Timescales
- **Age of the universe**: about **13.8 billion years**.
- **Age of the Earth**: about **4.5 billion years**.
- The universe is therefore about **3 times older** than Earth.

### What is the Big Bang theory?
- Our **best current theory** for how the universe began.
- States that ~13.8 billion years ago, the entire universe was **incredibly hot and dense**, concentrated in a tiny point.
- It then **expanded rapidly** and has been **expanding and cooling ever since**.
- ⚠️ It was **NOT** an explosion *in* space — it was an expansion *of* space itself.

### Evidence: the universe is expanding
- Astronomers (especially **Edwin Hubble**) found that **distant galaxies are moving away from us**.
- The **further away** a galaxy is, the **faster it appears to be receding** (moving away).
- This is detected through **redshift**: light from receding galaxies has its wavelength stretched, shifting it towards the red end of the spectrum.
- If everything is moving apart now, it must have been **closer together in the past** — leading back to the Big Bang.
